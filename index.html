About me

I'm a third year PhD student in the Department of Physics at The Ohio State University. I obtained my B.A. degree in physics from The College of Wooster in 2021, and M.S. degree in physics at Case Western Reserve University in 2023. I study the how a galaxy’s environment controls its ability to form stars—especially how satellite galaxies exchange and lose gas as they orbit within the circumgalactic medium of Milky Way–like hosts with my advisor Annika Peter. I have broad intrests include galaxy formation and evolution of dwarf galaxy, dark matter, the first star, and more.

I was born in Inner Mongolia, China. I love hiking, diving, climbing, crocheting, gardening, and more! I have two cutest cats in the world, Zaizai (崽崽) and Little Monkey (小猴).

Research

Here are a few research projects I'm currently working on.

Quenching

Simulations and observations to study how satellite galaxies lose gas in host halos. I use high-resolution cosmological simulations (Illustris-TNG50) to follow satellite galaxies as they move through the hot, diffuse circumgalactic medium (CGM) of Milky Way–like hosts. Ram pressure, tides, and gas accretion can remove or replenish a satellite’s cold gas, reshaping its star-formation history and enriching the host’s CGM. By connecting satellite orbits, CGM conditions, and gas budgets, I aim to identify when and how environment quenches satellites and how much material satellites return to their hosts.
